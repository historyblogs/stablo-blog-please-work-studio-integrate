RIP LOS ANGELES -- CONTENT EDITOR SETUP GUIDE


STEP 1: ACCEPT THE GITHUB INVITATION

Check your email for a message from GitHub with the subject line:
"You have been invited to collaborate on historyblogs/stablo-blog-please-work-studio-integrate"

Open that email and click the green Accept Invitation button.

You will need a GitHub account to accept. If you do not have one, go to:

https://github.com

...and create a free account first, then return to the invitation email and accept.


STEP 2: LOG IN TO THE EDITOR

Once you have accepted the GitHub invitation, open your browser and go to:

https://www.riplosangeles.com/keystatic

Click the Sign in with GitHub button.

GitHub will ask you to authorize the RIP Los Angeles CMS app. Click Authorize.

You will be brought to the editor dashboard. You should see:

   Hello, [your GitHub username]!
   Current branch: main
   Posts: 76 entries


STEP 3: EDITING A POST

1. Click Posts in the left sidebar.
2. Find the post you want to edit -- posts are listed alphabetically.
   Use your browser's Find (Cmd+F on Mac, Ctrl+F on Windows) to search.
3. Click the post title to open it.
4. Edit any fields -- Title, Date, Excerpt, Content, etc.
5. Click Save (top right corner) when done.

The live site updates within a minute or two after saving.


STEP 4: CREATING A NEW POST

1. Click Posts in the left sidebar.
2. Click Create entry (top right of the post list).
3. Fill in the Title -- this is required. Without a title the post will
   not build correctly.
4. Write your content.
5. Click Save when ready.


STEP 5: PUBLISHING A POST (DRAFT vs. LIVE)

When you create a new post, the Draft checkbox is ticked by default.
A draft post is saved but does not appear on the live site.

To publish a post:
1. Open the post in the editor.
2. Uncheck the Draft checkbox.
3. Click Save.
4. Wait about one minute, then visit riplosangeles.com to see it live.

If you want to make a change after publishing:
1. Go back to the post in the editor and make your edits.
2. Click Save. The live site updates in about a minute.

If you want to take a post down temporarily:
1. Open the post, check the Draft checkbox, and click Save.
2. It will disappear from the site within a minute.


NOTES

-- The Hero Image field will appear empty on older posts. This is fine --
   the images are there and display correctly on the live site. To replace
   a hero image, click the image field, upload a new photo, and save.

-- To add images inside the body of a post, click the image icon in the
   content toolbar and upload from your computer.

-- The editor URL is always:

https://www.riplosangeles.com/keystatic

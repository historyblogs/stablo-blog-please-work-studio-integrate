# RIP Los Angeles — Content Editor Guide

## How to Access the Editor

Open your browser and go to:

**https://www.riplosangeles.com/keystatic**

---

## Logging In

1. You will see a **"Sign in with GitHub"** button. Click it.
2. GitHub will ask you to authorize the RIP Los Angeles CMS app. Click **Authorize**.
3. You will be returned to the editor and land on the Dashboard.

> You only need to do the full GitHub authorization the first time. After that, logging in is just one click.

---

## The Editor Interface

On the left sidebar you will see:

- **Dashboard** — a welcome screen showing the current branch (always `main`)
- **Posts** — your list of all 76 posts

Click **Posts** to see the full list.

---

## Editing an Existing Post

1. Click **Posts** in the left sidebar.
2. Find the post you want — posts are listed alphabetically by title. You can also use your browser's built-in Find (Cmd+F) to search the page.
3. Click the post title to open it.
4. Edit any of the fields:
   - **Title** — the post headline
   - **Publish Date** — the original publication date
   - **Updated Date** — optional, leave blank if not applicable
   - **Draft** — tick this box to hide the post from the live site
   - **Excerpt** — the short summary shown on the blog index
   - **Hero Image** — the main image at the top of the post
   - **Category** — single category (e.g. *Personal Growth*)
   - **Tags** — add or remove tags one at a time
   - **Author** — defaults to Nathan Marsak
   - **Content** — the full body of the post (rich text editor)
5. When done, click the **Save** button (top right). This commits your changes directly to the site.

> The live site updates automatically within a minute or two after saving.

---

## Creating a New Post

1. Click **Posts** in the left sidebar.
2. Click the **Create entry** button (top right of the posts list).
3. Fill in the fields — Title is required; everything else is optional but recommended.
4. Write your post content in the **Content** area.
5. Click **Save** when ready.

---

## Adding Images to Post Content

Inside the content editor, click the **image icon** in the toolbar to insert an image. You can upload a photo directly from your computer. It will be stored in the site's image folder automatically.

---

## Hero Image Note

The hero image field shows blank for older posts — this is expected. The images are there and display correctly on the live site. If you want to update the hero image on an older post, simply click the image field, upload a new image, and save.

---

## Logging Out

Click your GitHub avatar or username in the top right corner of the editor and select **Sign out**.

The 2600 block of South Magnolia has some of the most magnificent houses in town.  A festival of two-story Edwardians, each house better than the next:



![2656 S Magnolia](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-02.webp)

*Look ma, no stucco!*

![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 2](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-03.webp)

 Ok, yes, there *is* one that's taller than two stories.  If you count a tower; that house looks, you know, like this:

![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 3](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-04.webp)

Soooo, you can *totally* understand why the YIMBYs would justifiably scream "this is the worst place on earth!  It is an affront to God-King Wiener!" and need to demolish *this* house smack-dab in the middle of the block—



![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 4](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-05.webp)

—which, when sold in 1905, was noted for its *flemish finish* and *artistic fixtures*—



![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 5](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-06.webp)

—and replace it with something twice as tall as anything else in the neighborhood:



![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 6](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-07.webp)

2656 Magnolia will be demolished by [beloved Orion Housing](https://live.staticflickr.com/65535/53164561169_f7a234ba00_o.jpg).  They've built about a dozen (and counting) bland boxes in favor of Old LA, so in this case, expect something similar:



![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 7](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-08.webp)

Reminds me of what Orion Housing did a couple blocks away on similarly bucolic 25th Street—



![2656 S Magnolia — photograph 8](~/assets/images/blog/2656-s-magnolia-09.webp)

—along with too many nearby neighborhood examples to speak of...though, speaking of, many of the best houses along Magnolia, specifically, are going or gone; I wrote [a bit about that here](https://www.riplosangeles.com/post/what-in-the-actual-hell-los-angeles) a couple years ago.



So long, West Adams!  You sure had something, once!
